This section gathers reference books and open-access technical resources.

---

## 1. Reference Books

> **Newman, J.; Thomas-Alyea, K. E.**
> *"Electrochemical Systems"*, 3rd Edition.
> Wiley-Interscience, **2004**.
> The bible of modern electrochemistry. Key chapters: 8 (Transport), 11 (Electrode Kinetics).
> [Internet Archive (Free borrowing)](https://archive.org/details/electrochemicals0000newm)

> **Schlesinger, M.; Paunovic, M.**
> *"Modern Electroplating"*, 5th Edition.
> Wiley, **2010**.
> Comprehensive industrial reference on plating processes.
> [Google Books (Limited preview)](https://books.google.com/books?id=modern+electroplating)

> **Bard, A. J.; Faulkner, L. R.**
> *"Electrochemical Methods"*, 2nd Edition.
> Wiley, **2001**.
> Theoretical fundamentals of analytical electrochemistry.

## 2. Free Courses and Tutorials

### Butler-Volmer Kinetics
> **MIT OpenCourseWare**
> *"Lecture 13: Electrode Kinetics - Butler-Volmer"*
> Course 10.626 (Electrochemical Energy Systems).
> Complete derivation of the Butler-Volmer equation with examples.
> [Course PDF (Free)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/10-626-electrochemical-energy-systems-spring-2014/resources/mit10_626s14_lec13/)

> **Chemistry LibreTexts**
> *"Butler-Volmer Equation"*
> Pedagogical explanation with thermodynamic derivation.
> [Read online (Free)](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Analytical_Chemistry/Supplemental_Modules_(Analytical_Chemistry)/Electrochemistry/Electrode_Kinetics)

### Current Distribution
> **COMSOL Multiphysics**
> *"Theory of Current Distribution"*
> Detailed documentation on primary, secondary, and tertiary distributions.
> [Online Documentation (Free)](https://www.comsol.com/multiphysics/current-distribution-theory)

> **BioLogic Learning Center**
> *"Current Distribution: Primary, Secondary and Tertiary"*
> Pedagogical application note on geometry and kinetics influence.
> [BioLogic Link (Free)](https://www.biologic.net/topics/what-is-current-distribution-primary-secondary-and-tertiary-distributions/)

### Finite Element Simulation
> **EchemFEM Documentation**
> *"Finite Element Methods for Electrochemistry"*
> Firedrake package for electrochemical simulation (code used in this project).
> [EchemFEM Documentation (Free)](https://echemfem.readthedocs.io/)
> [GitHub Source Code](https://github.com/LLNL/echemfem)
